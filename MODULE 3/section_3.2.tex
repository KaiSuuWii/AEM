\subsection{Right-Hand Rule and Orientation}

\subsubsection{Direction of the Cross Product}

\begin{conceptbox}
    The direction of $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$ is determined by the \textbf{Right-Hand Rule}:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Point your right-hand fingers in the direction of $\mathbf{a}$,
        \item curl them toward $\mathbf{b}$ through the smaller angle,
        \item your thumb points in the direction of $\mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b}$.
    \end{itemize}
\end{conceptbox}

This means the order matters:
\[
    \mathbf{a}\times\mathbf{b} \;\text{points opposite to}\; \mathbf{b}\times\mathbf{a}.
\]

\subsubsection{Right-Handed Coordinate Systems}

In a standard right-handed Cartesian coordinate system, the unit basis vectors satisfy:
\[
    \mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{j}=\mathbf{k},\quad
    \mathbf{j}\times\mathbf{k}=\mathbf{i},\quad
    \mathbf{k}\times\mathbf{i}=\mathbf{j}.
\]

Reversing the order flips the sign:
\[
    \mathbf{j}\times\mathbf{i}=-\mathbf{k},\quad
    \mathbf{k}\times\mathbf{j}=-\mathbf{i},\quad
    \mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{k}=-\mathbf{j}.
\]

\begin{examplebox}
    Compute $\mathbf{j}\times\mathbf{i}$.

    Using the right-handed relationships:
    \[
        \mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{j}=\mathbf{k}
        \quad\Rightarrow\quad
        \mathbf{j}\times\mathbf{i}=-(\mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{j})=-\mathbf{k}.
    \]
\end{examplebox}

\begin{noteBox}
    Engineering meaning: In torque and rotational motion, changing the order of vectors changes the rotation direction (clockwise vs counterclockwise), so orientation is physically important.
\end{noteBox}
