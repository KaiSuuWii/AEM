\subsection{Linear Systems in Matrix Form}

Consider the system:

\[
    \begin{aligned}
        2x_1 + x_2  & = 5 \\
        4x_1 - 3x_2 & = 1
    \end{aligned}
\]

We write it in matrix form as:

\[
    Ax = b
\]

where

\[
    A =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        2 & 1  \\
        4 & -3
    \end{bmatrix},
    \quad
    x =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        x_1 \\
        x_2
    \end{bmatrix},
    \quad
    b =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        5 \\
        1
    \end{bmatrix}.
\]

\begin{conceptbox}
    The equation $Ax = b$ represents a system of linear equations.
\end{conceptbox}

\begin{itemize}
    \item If $b = 0$, the system is \textbf{homogeneous}.
    \item If $b \neq 0$, the system is \textbf{nonhomogeneous}.
\end{itemize}
