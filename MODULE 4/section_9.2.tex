\subsection{Orthogonal Projection Interpretation}

The vector $Ax$ lies in the column space of $A$.

If $b$ is not in the column space,
we project $b$ onto $\text{Col}(A)$.

\begin{conceptbox}
    The least squares solution makes the error vector
    \[
        e = b - Ax
    \]
    orthogonal to $\text{Col}(A)$.
\end{conceptbox}

Thus:

\[
    A^T (b - Ax) = 0.
\]

Geometrically:

\begin{itemize}
    \item $Ax$ is the orthogonal projection of $b$.
    \item The residual is perpendicular to the column space.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Projection Formula}

If columns of $A$ are independent:

\[
    \hat{x} = (A^T A)^{-1} A^T b.
\]
