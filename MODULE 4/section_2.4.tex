\subsection{Complex Eigenvalues}

Eigenvalues of real matrices may be complex.

\subsubsection*{Example}

Consider:

\[
    A =
    \begin{bmatrix}
        0 & -1 \\
        1 & 0
    \end{bmatrix}.
\]

Compute:

\[
    \det(A - \lambda I)
    =
    \begin{vmatrix}
        -\lambda & -1       \\
        1        & -\lambda
    \end{vmatrix}
    =
    \lambda^2 + 1.
\]

Solve:

\[
    \lambda^2 + 1 = 0
\]

\[
    \lambda = \pm i.
\]

\begin{conceptbox}
    Complex eigenvalues of real matrices occur in conjugate pairs.
\end{conceptbox}

\subsubsection*{Geometric Meaning}

The matrix

\[
    \begin{bmatrix}
        0 & -1 \\
        1 & 0
    \end{bmatrix}
\]

\begin{itemize}
    \item No real invariant directions exist.
    \item The transformation corresponds to rotation.
\end{itemize}

\begin{noteBox}
    If eigenvalues are:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item $\lambda = a \pm bi$, the transformation represents rotation combined with scaling.
        \item $|\lambda| = 1$, pure rotation.
    \end{itemize}
\end{noteBox}
