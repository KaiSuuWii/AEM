
\subsection*{1.7 Vector Functions in Engineering Applications}

Vector functions model trajectories, fluid motion, and signal propagation paths.

\begin{examplebox}
    The vector function
    \begin{align*}
        \mathbf{r}(t)=\langle \cos t, \sin t, t\rangle
    \end{align*}
    represents a helix. The projection onto the $xy$-plane is circular motion, while $z=t$ introduces linear growth, forming a spiral trajectory common in electromagnetic wave propagation.
\end{examplebox}

\begin{examplebox}
    In signal processing, parametric curves such as:
    \begin{align*}
        \mathbf{r}(t)=\langle x(t), y(t) \rangle
    \end{align*}
    can represent phase-space trajectories of signals, where $x(t)$ is the signal and $y(t)$ its derivative, enabling analysis of system dynamics.
\end{examplebox}
