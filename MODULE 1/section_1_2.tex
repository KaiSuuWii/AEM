\subsection*{1.2 Divergence of Series}

A series $\sum a_n$ is said to \textbf{diverge} if the sequence of partial sums does not approach a finite limit.

\paragraph{Key Divergence Tests}
\begin{itemize}
    \item $n$th-Term Test for Divergence
    \item Harmonic Series
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Example 1: $n$th-Term Test}
\[
    \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n}{n+1}
\]
Since
\[
    \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{n}{n+1} = 1 \neq 0,
\]
the series diverges.

\paragraph{Example 2: Harmonic Series}
\[
    \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n}
\]
This series diverges even though $a_n \to 0$.

\paragraph{Example 3: Comparison}
\[
    \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}
\]
This is a $p$-series with $p = \frac{1}{2} < 1$, hence it diverges.
